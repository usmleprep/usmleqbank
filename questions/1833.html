Researchers conduct a study in an animal fetus with circulation similar to that of humans. The fetus is delivered close to term by cesarean delivery. Prior to placental separation and the initiation of pulmonary respiration, blood samples are simultaneously collected from several different fetal blood vessels. The blood oxygen saturations measured from each of the samples are 71%, 64%, 62%, 55%, and 43%. The sample with the greatest oxygen saturation was most likely obtained from which of the following blood vessels?

A. Descending aorta
B. Ductus arteriosus
C. Inferior vena cava
D. Pulmonary trunk
E. Superior vena cava
F. Umbilical artery

    • Explanation

    Fetal circulation differs considerably from adult circulation in that oxygen is supplied to the fetus by the mother's placenta, no gas exchange occurs in the fetal lungs, and the majority of blood is directed away from the lungs via right-to-left shunting (eg, foramen ovale, ductus arteriosus).

    Oxygenated blood from the placenta is delivered to the fetus via the umbilical vein. Upon entering the fetal abdomen, a portion of this oxygen-rich blood is directed to the portal vein to supply the liver, while the majority is shunted through the ductus venosus to the inferior vena cava (IVC). In the IVC, oxygen-rich blood from the ductus venosus runs alongside a stream of deoxygenated blood returning from the lower body.

    As blood from the IVC enters the right atrium, a preferential streaming effect allows the majority of the oxygenated blood to pass directly through the foramen ovale into the left atrium. The oxygen-rich blood delivered to the left atrium proceeds to the left ventricle and out the aorta, where it is supplied to the brain, myocardium (via the coronary arteries), and upper extremities.

    Meanwhile, deoxygenated blood from the IVC and superior vena cava fills the right atrium and proceeds to the right ventricle. High pulmonary vascular resistance causes most of the blood ejected into the pulmonary trunk to be shunted away from the lungs, with 90% of the deoxygenated blood being shunted through the ductus arteriosus into the aorta (Choices B and D). This deoxygenated blood mixes with the oxygen-rich blood in the aorta, sending moderately oxygenated blood down the descending aorta to supply the intestines, kidneys, and lower body.

    (Choice A) Oxygen saturation in the descending aorta is significantly lower than in the ductus venosus because the oxygenated blood in the descending aorta is mixed with deoxygenated blood from both the lower and the upper body.

    (Choice E) The superior vena cava carries deoxygenated blood returning from the brain and upper extremities.

    (Choice F) The umbilical arteries branch off the internal iliac arteries to return moderately oxygenated blood to the placenta, where carbon dioxide and other wastes are unloaded and oxygen is again collected.

    Educational objective:

    The most highly oxygenated blood in the fetus is carried by the umbilical vein, which empties directly into the inferior vena cava via the ductus venosus.
