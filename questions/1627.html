A 60-year-old man is being evaluated due to nontender cervical lymphadenopathy that he first noticed while shaving. A biopsy is performed, and microscopy findings are shown in the image below:

Further analysis would most likely demonstrate which of the following?

A. BCL2 overexpression
B. BCR-ABL1 fusion
C. HER2 overexpression
D. MYC overexpression
E. PML-RARA fusion

    B. BCR-ABL1 fusion (10%)
    C. HER2 overexpression (1%)
    D. MYC overexpression (20%)
    E. PML-RARA fusion (1%)

    • Explanation

    This patient with painless lymphadenopathy has histopathologic features consistent with follicular lymphoma (FL), the most common indolent form of non-Hodgkin lymphoma. FL is a mature B-cell tumor derived from cells found in the germinal centers of lymph nodes.

    At low magnification, the characteristic histopathologic feature of FL is the presence of nodular follicles that obscure the normal lymph node architecture. In contrast to normal reactive lymph nodes, the neoplastic follicles are closely packed and similar in size. Instead of being polarized into dark and light zones, they consist of a mixture of centrocytes (small, cleaved cells) and centroblasts (larger, noncleaved cells).

    Approximately 85% of FL is associated with the t(14;18) translocation, which results in overexpression of the antiapoptotic gene product BCL2 (allows the neoplastic cells to evade apoptosis).

    (Choice B) Chronic myeloid leukemia (CML), a myeloproliferative neoplasm, is associated with a translocation involving the BCR gene on chromosome 22 and the ABL1 gene on chromosome 9 [t(9;22)], resulting in a BCR-ABL1 fusion protein that leads to uncontrolled cellular proliferation. CML is associated with granulocyte proliferation, which dramatically increases the quantity of granulocytes in the bone marrow and blood. Abnormal lymphoid follicles in a lymph node would not be expected.

    (Choice C) HER2 is a receptor tyrosine kinase that promotes cell growth. In some cases of breast carcinoma, the HER2 gene is amplified, causing HER2 overexpression and increased cellular proliferation. Histopathology of metastatic breast carcinoma in a lymph node is typically similar to that of the primary tumor (eg, atypical epithelial cells forming glands).

    (Choice D) Burkitt lymphoma is an aggressive non-Hodgkin lymphoma associated with translocations of the MYC gene on chromosome 8, usually onto the immunoglobulin heavy-chain region of chromosome 14 [t(8;14)], resulting in MYC overexpression. Histologically, Burkitt lymphoma shows a diffuse, not nodular, proliferation of medium-sized lymphoid cells with numerous mitotic figures and scattered benign macrophages (ie, "starry sky" appearance).

    (Choice E) Acute promyelocytic leukemia is associated with a translocation involving the retinoic acid receptor alpha (RARA) and promyelocytic leukemia (PML) genes. The PML-RARA fusion protein product inhibits differentiation of myeloid precursors. Microscopic examination of blood and bone marrow typically shows abnormal promyelocytes with multiple cytoplasmic Auer rods.

    Educational objective:

    Follicular lymphoma is histologically characterized by neoplastic follicles that obscure the normal lymph node architecture. Most cases have the t(14;18) translocation, which causes overexpression of the antiapoptotic BCL2 protein.
