Molecular biologists undertake a series of experiments designed to classify proteins involved in various intracellular pathways in neurons. During one of the experiments, a protein mixture obtained from a cell culture is separated by gel electrophoresis and subsequently transferred to a filter membrane. Labeled probes consisting of double-strand DNA are then used to detect a specific target of interest in the sample. This method is most likely to detect which of the following?

A. Cell surface receptors
B. Kinesins
C. Ribosomes
D. Spliceosomes
E. Transcription factors

    Blot techniques
    Technique Substance detected Type of probe
    Northern RNA Single-strand DNA or RNA
    Southern DNA
    Western Protein Antibody
    Southwestern DNA-binding protein Double-strand DNA

    The Northern, Southern, and Western blot techniques can be used to identify RNA, DNA, and protein, respectively; the Southwestern blot technique specifically detects DNA-binding proteins. In all analysis methods, the unknown specimen is separated according to molecular weight and charge by gel electrophoresis. Once separated, the molecules form bands that are blotted onto a nitrocellulose membrane and incubated with a labeled probe specific to the specimen being analyzed.

    In this experiment, the labeled probe is double-stranded DNA, which is used in the Southwestern blot technique to identify DNA-binding proteins (in contrast to single-strand nucleic acid hybridization probes used in Northern and Southern blots). The probe attaches to the DNA-binding region of the protein (eg, zinc finger, leucine zipper, Rel homology domain). Of the options listed, only transcription factors bind DNA and would therefore be detected by double-stranded DNA probes.

    (Choice A) Cell surface receptors bind extracellular ligands (eg, neurotransmitters).

    (Choice B) Kinesins are proteins involved in transporting intracellular products from the center to the periphery of a cell (eg, axonal transport). Kinesins have binding sites for microtubules and ATP, not DNA.

    (Choice C) Ribosomes are the sites of protein synthesis. They bind mRNA, not DNA.

    (Choice D) Spliceosomes generate mature mRNA by binding pre-mRNA to remove introns. Spliceosomes do not bind DNA.

    Educational objective:

    Double-strand DNA probes are used in the Southwestern blot technique to identify DNA-binding proteins, including transcription factors.
