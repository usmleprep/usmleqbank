A 60-year-old man comes to the office due to persistent cough for the past several weeks. He reports producing minimal sputum that recently has contained occasional specks of blood. The patient also has anorexia and has lost 7 kg (15.4 lb) over the past 2 months. He has no previous medical conditions and takes no medications. The patient has smoked a pack of cigarettes daily for 40 years and drinks alcohol on social occasions. Temperature is 37.1 C (98.7 F), blood pressure is 130/80 mm Hg, pulse is 72/min, and respirations are 16/min. On physical examination, respirations are unlabored but with occasional wheezes on the left side. The remainder of the examination shows no abnormalities. X-ray of the chest reveals a hilar mass with adenopathy on the left side. Laboratory results show a decreased serum sodium level. Which of the following additional laboratory abnormalities are most likely to be present in this patient?

    • Explanation

    This patient has hyponatremia associated with a left-sided lung mass. These findings likely represent the syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone secretion (SIADH), which can occur with certain tumors (eg, small cell lung carcinoma, which occurs mostly in smokers) due to ectopic production of antidiuretic hormone (ADH).

    ADH secretion from the posterior pituitary is stimulated by high plasma osmolality (eg, water deprivation) or low extracellular volume. ADH increases water reabsorption in the renal collecting ducts, increasing blood volume and restoring serum osmolality to normal levels (which suppresses further ADH secretion). In SIADH, unregulated ADH secretion causes the following changes:

    • Hyponatremia and low serum osmolality from excess water retention
    • Concentrated urine with high urine osmolality from reduced renal excretion of water
    • High urine sodium concentration results from increased secretion of natriuretic peptides due to increased extracellular fluid volume expansion

    Initially, SIADH may cause a transient volume expansion; however, subsequent natriuresis (sodium and water excretion) restores the extracellular volume to normal, and patients are clinically euvolemic (eg, normotension, normal jugular venous pressure).

    Educational objective:

    Ectopic production of antidiuretic hormone (ADH) from malignancy can lead to the syndrome of inappropriate ADH secretion, a condition of impaired urinary water excretion. This condition manifests with low serum osmolality, hyponatremia, high urine osmolality, and high urine sodium concentration.
