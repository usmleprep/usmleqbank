A newborn is taken to the neonatal intensive care unit after delivery due to respiratory distress. The neonate was born full-term, and there were no significant complications during pregnancy or delivery. Once the patient is stabilized, a detailed physical examination is performed and shows an underdeveloped mandible and hypoplastic zygomatic bones. Genetic testing confirms a gene mutation that results in abnormal development of the first and second pharyngeal arches. Which of the following structures is also likely abnormal in this patient?

A. Cricoid cartilage
B. Glossopharyngeal nerve
C. Greater horn of hyoid
D. Stapes
E. Thyroid cartilage
F. Vagus nerve

    • Explanation

    The developing embryo has 6 pharyngeal arches, and all but the fifth arch contribute to adult structures. The mesoderm forms muscular and vascular elements, and neural crest cells form bony and cartilaginous structures. Each arch is also innervated by a specific cranial nerve (CN).

    • The first arch is associated with the trigeminal nerve (CN V). Its bony derivatives include the maxilla, zygoma, mandible, incus, and malleus, and its muscular derivatives include the muscles of mastication (eg, masseter, temporalis).
    • The second arch is associated with the facial nerve (CN VII) and gives rise to the styloid process of the temporal bone, lesser horn of the hyoid, and stapes. Muscular derivatives include the muscles of facial expression.

    This patient's presentation is consistent with Treacher Collins syndrome, a rare genetic disorder resulting in the abnormal development of the first and second pharyngeal arches. The associated craniofacial abnormalities (eg, mandibular, maxillary, and zygomatic bone hypoplasia) often result in airway compromise, while absent or abnormal ossicles (eg, incus, malleus, stapes) can lead to profound conductive hearing loss.

    (Choices A, E, and F) The fourth and sixth arches make up the cartilaginous structures of the larynx, including the cricoid and thyroid cartilages. Derivatives of these arches (eg, most pharyngeal and laryngeal muscles) are innervated by the vagus nerve (CN X).

    (Choice B) The third pharyngeal arch is innervated by the glossopharyngeal nerve (CN IX). Neurologic dysfunction may lead to impaired taste perception and an absent gag reflex.

    (Choice C) Although the lesser horn of the hyoid bone is derived from the secondary pharyngeal arch, the greater horn of the hyoid is derived from the third arch.

    Educational objective:

    Disrupted development of the first and second pharyngeal arches results in hypoplasia of the arches' musculoskeletal derivatives, including the mandible, maxilla, zygomatic bones, and ossicles.
