A 64-year-old man is being evaluated for nausea and a new-onset headache that worsens when he bends over. Brain imaging shows a hemispheric cerebral mass that is subsequently biopsied. Light microscopy of the tissue specimen is shown in the image below.

Which of the following histologic findings is most characteristic of this lesion?

A. Cyst formation and rare mitoses
B. Inflammatory cell infiltrate and reticulin deposits
C. Microscopic laminar calcium deposition
D. Necrosis and vascular proliferation
E. Proliferating cells surrounding blood vessels

    B. Inflammatory cell infiltrate and reticulin deposits (14%)
    C. Microscopic laminar calcium deposition (3%)
    D. Necrosis and vascular proliferation (57%)
    E. Proliferating cells surrounding blood vessels (21%)

    • Explanation

    This image shows an area of necrosis surrounded by columns of tumor cells (ie, pseudopalisading necrosis) with capillaries at the periphery (vascular proliferation); the central necrotic area represents a vascular insult resulting from haphazard tumor growth, with the surviving tumor cells forming columns as they migrate toward the peripheral vasculature. These histologic features are highly characteristic of glioblastoma multiforme (GBM), a malignant glioma that is the most common primary brain neoplasm in adults.

    GBM is usually located within the cerebral hemispheres (eg, frontal lobe, temporal lobe) and often presents with new-onset headache that worsens with bending over (increases intracranial pressure). Nausea/vomiting, focal neurological defects, and/or seizures are also frequently seen. The prognosis for patients with GBM is poor, with median survival at 10-12 months.

    (Choice A) Cyst formation and rare mitoses indicate a colloid cyst, a pathologically benign tumor usually found in the third ventricle. Although colloid cysts are usually asymptomatic, they can cause lethal obstructive hydrocephalus.

    (Choice B) Reticulin deposits and chronic inflammatory infiltrates are seen in pleomorphic xanthoastrocytomas. Although they can rarely progress to GBM, these tumors are usually found in children and young adults.

    (Choice C) Meningiomas are tumors of the meninges that have a whorled pattern of growth. Although they are not always present, laminar calcifications called psammoma bodies are characteristic of benign meningiomas.

    (Choice E) Ependymomas also display prominent vascular proliferation. However, these tumors are usually infratentorial (eg, fourth ventricle, spinal cord), rare in adults, and characterized histologically by proliferating ependymal cells surrounding blood vessels (perivascular pseudorosettes) rather than prominent areas of necrosis.

    Educational objective:

    Glioblastoma multiforme is the most common primary brain tumor in adults. Histopathology showing increased vascularity and pseudopalisading tumor cells around areas of necrosis is highly characteristic.
